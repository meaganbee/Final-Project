Immigrant Communities in New York City

New York City is home to large and ethnically diverse immigrant communities, with 3.2 million people identified as foreign-born according to the latest report from the NYC Mayor's Office of Immigrant Affairs.

The borough of Queens is home to the largest immigrant population in the city with 1.1 million people. In order to better serve vulnerable immigrant populations, the below map shows service providers throughout Queens and the type of assistance they offer.

Communities are dispersed across a number of different neighborhoods in Queens, including:

  • Jackson Heights
  • Flushing
  • Jamaica
  • Elmhurst
  • Astoria

The countries represented in Queens include (but definitely aren't limited to):

China
India
Jamaica

More broadly, regions represented include:

South Central Asia
Caribbean
South America

This map provides general services for immigrants from all countries and ethnicities.
